Astrophysicists are puzzled by 'little red dots' observed by the James Webb Space Telescope, which may be black hole stars or other exotic objects, challenging existing models of early galaxy formation. These objects could represent a completely new class of cosmic object, forcing a rethink of how galaxies and black holes formed in the early universe. The 'little red dots' appear to have existed between 0.6 and 1.6 billion years after the Big Bang, and recent theories suggest they could be quasi-stars or black hole stars—hypothetical objects with a black hole core inside a massive envelope.

**Background**: The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the most powerful space telescope ever built, capable of observing the earliest galaxies. 'Little red dots' are a class of small, red-tinted objects discovered by JWST in 2024, whose nature remains unclear. A quasi-star, or black hole star, is a hypothetical type of extremely massive star that may have existed early in the universe, with a black hole at its core.

<summary><span class="hz-item-title">Prompt injection leaks private YouTube videos</span> <span class="hz-item-score">⭐️ 9.0/10</span></summary>

A security researcher demonstrated that prompt injection in YouTube's AI comment reply feature can leak creators' private video titles and metadata. The attack works when a creator clicks a suggested AI reply to a malicious comment. This vulnerability exposes private videos of millions of creators, undermining YouTube's privacy guarantees. It highlights the broader risk of prompt injection in AI-integrated platforms, where user input can manipulate model behavior to leak sensitive data. The injection payload is placed in a comment; when the creator uses YouTube's suggested AI reply, the model executes the injected instruction and returns private video titles. The researcher reported the bug to Google in August 2024, but it was initially classified as a low-severity 'spam' issue.

🔗 [Source](https://javoriuski.com/post/youtube)

hackernews · javxfps · Jul 4, 16:45 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48786781)

**Background**: Prompt injection is a security exploit where malicious inputs cause AI models to behave unintentionally. YouTube's AI comment reply feature suggests responses to comments, but it processes user comments as part of the prompt, allowing attackers to inject instructions that override the model's intended behavior.
